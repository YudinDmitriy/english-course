  • Short description: This course is designed for Intermediate (B1) and higher English language learners seeking to improve their skills in understanding and analyzing English-language articles, develop critical thinking and confidence in working with textual information. The course uses authentic articles from a variety of sources covering a wide range of topics and offers practical exercises to consolidate the knowledge gained.
  • The purpose of the course is to develop comprehensive skills in working with English-language articles, including understanding, analyzing, critical thinking, and using information to create your own texts.
  • Course sections:
    • Foundations of Article Comprehension
    • Critical Analysis & Contextual Understanding
    • Applying Knowledge & Creative Expression
  • Duration: 12 lessons (18 hours)
  • Target Audience: learners with level B1 and above
  • Evaluation:
    • Active participation in classes (30%)
    • Weekly written assignments (analysis of articles, answers to questions) (40%)
    • Final project (creation of your own article/blog in English) (30%)
  • Forms of work:

  • Home-Reading: Students independently read and analyze various articles
  • Vocabulary Quizzes: Regular quizzes to test understanding of key vocabulary words from the articles.
  • Article Summaries: Students write concise summaries of articles to demonstrate comprehension of the main points.
  • Critical Analysis Essays: Students analyze the article's arguments, biases, and evidence
  • Reflection Journals: Students write regular reflections on their learning process and challenges.
  • Vocabulary Journal/Log: Students maintain a personal vocabulary log with new words learned from the articles, along with definitions and example sentences.
  • Annotating Articles:Students use online annotation tools (like Hypothesis) to highlight key passages and add comments directly to the article.

  • Online Discussions: Students participate in online discussion forums to share their thoughts and insights on the articles.
  • Small Group Article Presentations:Students work in small groups to present summaries and analyses of the articles to the class.
  • Peer Review:Students provide feedback on each other's summaries, essays, or other written assignments.
  • Debates: Students engage in online debates about controversial topics raised in the articles.
  • Collaborative Annotation: Students collaboratively annotate articles together using a tool like Hypothesis.
  • Jigsaw Reading: Divide an article into sections and assign each section to a different student. Students become experts on their assigned sections and then teach the content to other group members.

  • Live Q&A Sessions: Live sessions where students can ask the instructor questions about the articles and course material.
  • Online Polls and Quizzes: Quick online polls and quizzes to check understanding and encourage participation.
  • Interactive Whiteboard Activities: Using online whiteboardtools (like Miro or Google Jamboard) for brainstorming, mind mapping, or collaborative problem-solving.
  • Drag-and-Drop Activities: Matching vocabulary words to definitions or ordering events chronologically.

  • Create a Blog: Students create their own blog and write article reviews or opinion pieces on a regular basis.
  • Produce a Podcast/Video:Students create a podcast or video discussing and analyzing an article.
  • Infographic Creation: Students create an infographic to visualize key data and concepts from an article.
  • Course training material: Authentic articles from: The New York Times, The Guardian, BBC, Scientific American, Harvard Business Review, etc., online dictionaries, grammar reference books, interactive platforms (Quizlet, Padlet)
